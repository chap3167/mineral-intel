Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about MineralSearch.

What is MineralSearch?

MineralSearch is an oil and gas data platform covering Texas onshore and Gulf of Mexico offshore. It provides well data, production history, drilling permits, lease information, operator details, and mineral ownership data for over 1.3 million wells.

How many wells does MineralSearch have?

MineralSearch has data on over 1.3 million wells in Texas, covering all 275 counties. This includes 281,000+ producing wells, 41,000+ operators, and 817,000+ completions.

Where does the data come from?

Our data comes directly from the Railroad Commission of Texas (RRC), the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM), the Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement (BSEE), and county appraisal district records. It is updated monthly.

How often is the data updated?

Well data, production data, and permit data are updated monthly from the Railroad Commission of Texas. Gulf of Mexico data is updated from BOEM and BSEE on a similar schedule.

Is MineralSearch a good alternative to Enverus?

Yes. MineralSearch provides similar Texas well data, production, and permit information at a fraction of the cost. While Enverus targets large enterprises at $10,000-$50,000+ per year, MineralSearch is built for independent landmen and small operators at an accessible price point.

What can I search for?

You can search by county, operator name, lease name, API number, well type, and more. The platform supports filtering by production status, well type, completion date, and geographic area.

Do you have Gulf of Mexico offshore data?

Yes. MineralSearch includes Gulf of Mexico offshore well data from BOEM and BSEE, including lease information, production data, and operator details.

Do you have mineral ownership data?

Yes. We pull mineral ownership data from county appraisal districts (CADs) across Texas. This includes owner names, mineral interest percentages, and property descriptions.

Can I export data?

Yes. Paid subscribers can export well data, production data, and other records in CSV format for use in spreadsheets and analysis tools.

Who is MineralSearch built for?

MineralSearch is built for landmen, mineral buyers, small operators, royalty owners, and anyone who needs access to Texas oil and gas data without paying enterprise prices.
